Welcome to Your Business Dining Success Guide

Are you nervous about your first business lunch or dinner? You're not alone! Many UA students have little to no experience with business dining situations, but mastering these skills is crucial for your professional success.

This website provides essential guidance for University of Arizona students who want to navigate business meals with confidence. Whether you're attending a job interview over lunch, networking at a formal dinner, or meeting with potential clients, knowing proper dining etiquette will help you make a positive impression.

Why Business Dining Etiquette Matters

Business meals aren't just about food—they're opportunities to build relationships and showcase your professionalism. According to a survey by the Society for Human Resource Management, 75% of recruiters consider dining manners to be a significant factor in their hiring decisions.

Essential Etiquette Guide

Before the Meal

  • Preparation: Research the restaurant in advance. Arrive 5-10 minutes early.
  • Seating: Wait to be seated or follow your host's lead.
  • Napkin: Place your napkin on your lap shortly after being seated.
  • Ordering: Take cues from your host. Order mid-priced items that are easy to eat.

During the Meal

  • Utensil Selection: Work from the outside in. The forks on the left, knives and spoons on the right.
  • Bread Etiquette: Break bread into small pieces and butter them individually.
  • Pace: Match your eating speed with others at the table.
  • Resting Position: Cross your utensils on your plate to indicate you're resting, place them parallel at 4 o'clock position to show you're finished.

Conversation Tips

  • Focus on building relationships rather than closing deals.
  • Safe topics include travel, hobbies, industry news, and shared interests.
  • Avoid controversial subjects like politics, religion, and personal problems.
  • Ask thoughtful questions and practice active listening.

After the Meal

  • The person who extended the invitation typically pays.
  • Express sincere thanks to your host.
  • Send a follow-up email or note within 24 hours.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Top Business Dining Faux Pas

  1. Using your phone during the meal
  2. Ordering messy or difficult-to-eat foods
  3. Beginning to eat before everyone is served
  4. Discussing business too early in the meal
  5. Ordering alcohol when others aren't drinking
  6. Talking with food in your mouth
  7. Reaching across the table instead of asking for items to be passed
  8. Arguing over the bill

How to Recover Gracefully

Everyone makes mistakes occasionally. If you commit a faux pas:

  • Offer a brief, sincere apology without drawing excessive attention
  • Move on quickly and don't dwell on the error
  • Use humor appropriately if it fits the situation
  • Focus on making the rest of the interaction positive

Cultural Considerations

Business dining etiquette varies significantly across cultures. When dining with international colleagues:

  • Research specific cultural customs before the meal
  • Observe others and follow their lead
  • Be open to trying new foods and customs
  • Ask respectful questions if you're unsure
